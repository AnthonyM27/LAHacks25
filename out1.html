Berkeley Artificial Intelligence Research (BAIR) Lab

Mission/Focus: To bring together UC Berkeley researchers across various domains (computer vision, machine learning, natural language processing, planning, control, and robotics) to define the foundations of AI and explore its applications.

Key AI Areas:

  • Machine Learning (Deep Learning, Reinforcement Learning, Theory)
  • Computer Vision
  • Natural Language Processing (NLP)
  • Robotics (Control, Planning, Perception, Human-Robot Interaction)
  • AI Security and Privacy
  • Fairness, Accountability, and Transparency in AI

Notable Faculty/Leaders: Pieter Abbeel, Anca Dragan, Trevor Darrell, Alexei A. Efros, Michael I. Jordan, Sergey Levine, Jitendra Malik, Stuart Russell, Dawn Song, Ion Stoica (Note: Many affiliated faculty)

Major Projects/Labs/Collaborations: BAIR integrates research from many individual faculty labs. Hosts seminars, industry partnerships, and publishes a widely read blog. Many foundational AI models and algorithms originate from BAIR-affiliated researchers.

Website: https://bair.berkeley.edu/

Center for Human-Compatible AI (CHAI)

Mission/Focus: To develop the conceptual and technical foundations for provably beneficial AI systems, ensuring AI systems remain safe and aligned with human values as they become more intelligent.

Key AI Areas:

  • AI Safety
  • Value Alignment
  • Preference Learning
  • Provably Beneficial Systems
  • Cooperative AI
  • Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback

Notable Faculty/Leaders: Stuart Russell (Director), Anca Dragan, Pieter Abbeel, Tom Griffiths, Jelani Nelson

Major Projects/Labs/Collaborations: Research focuses on theoretical frameworks and algorithms for safe AI, often involving collaboration across philosophy, economics, and computer science.

Website: https://humancompatible.ai/

Berkeley DeepDrive (BDD)

Mission/Focus: To pioneer technologies for automotive applications, focusing on computer vision and machine learning for autonomous driving.

Key AI Areas:

  • Computer Vision (for autonomous driving)
  • Deep Learning
  • Sensor Fusion
  • Perception for Autonomous Systems
  • Transfer Learning

Notable Faculty/Leaders: Trevor Darrell, Kurt Keutzer, Ken Goldberg

Major Projects/Labs/Collaborations: Industry consortium with major automotive and tech companies. Known for the BDD100K dataset, one of the largest and most diverse driving video datasets.

Website: https://bdd.berkeley.edu/

AUTOLAB (Automation Sciences Lab)

Mission/Focus: Research in robotics and automation, including robotic manipulation, perception, and learning, with applications ranging from manufacturing and logistics to healthcare and surgery.

Key AI Areas:

  • Robotics (Manipulation, Grasping, Planning)
  • Machine Learning for Robotics (Reinforcement Learning, Imitation Learning)
  • Computer Vision for Robotics
  • Automation Systems
  • Cloud Robotics

Notable Faculty/Leaders: Ken Goldberg

Major Projects/Labs/Collaborations: Research on Dex-Net (robust robotic grasping), surgical robotics, cloud robotics platforms. Collaborations with industry and medical institutions.

Website: https://autolab.berkeley.edu/

Berkeley NLP Group

Mission/Focus: To research and develop computational methods for understanding and generating human language, covering a wide spectrum of NLP tasks.

Key AI Areas:

  • Natural Language Processing (Syntax, Semantics, Pragmatics)
  • Machine Translation
  • Question Answering
  • Information Extraction
  • Computational Linguistics
  • Deep Learning for NLP

Notable Faculty/Leaders: Dan Klein, Marti Hearst, David Bamman

Major Projects/Labs/Collaborations: Development of core NLP models and techniques, often contributing to widely used toolkits and benchmarks. Research spans foundational linguistic understanding to practical applications.

Website: https://nlp.cs.berkeley.edu/

Berkeley Vision and Learning Center (BVLC)

Mission/Focus: Research at the intersection of computer vision and machine learning, developing algorithms for visual understanding and generating visual content.

Key AI Areas:

  • Computer Vision (Object Recognition, Scene Understanding, Activity Recognition)
  • Machine Learning (Deep Learning, Unsupervised Learning)
  • Computational Photography
  • Visual Reasoning
  • Generative Models for Vision

Notable Faculty/Leaders: Trevor Darrell, Jitendra Malik, Alexei A. Efros, Ren Ng, Angjoo Kanazawa

Major Projects/Labs/Collaborations: Historically known for developing the Caffe deep learning framework. Continues research in foundational vision tasks, deep learning architectures, and multimodal learning (vision+language).

Website: https://bvlc.eecs.berkeley.edu/

Sky Computing Lab (Successor to RISELab)

Mission/Focus: To develop technologies for cloud computing that enable efficient, secure, and reliable execution of diverse applications, including large-scale AI/ML workloads, across multiple cloud environments ("Sky Computing"). Builds upon the legacy of RISELab (Real-time Intelligence with Secure Execution).

Key AI Areas:

  • Systems for Machine Learning (Distributed Training, Inference Serving)
  • Secure Computing for AI
  • Data Systems for AI
  • Cloud Computing infrastructure for AI
  • Serverless Computing for AI

Notable Faculty/Leaders: Ion Stoica, Joseph Gonzalez, Raluca Ada Popa, Natacha Crooks

Major Projects/Labs/Collaborations: Developed popular open-source systems like Ray (distributed computing), Skyplane (cloud data transfer). Strong industry collaborations related to cloud infrastructure and AI systems.

Website: https://sky.cs.berkeley.edu/

CITRIS and the Banatao Institute (Center for Information Technology Research in the Interest of Society)

Mission/Focus: While broader than just AI, CITRIS leverages information technology (including AI) for social good, focusing on areas like health, sustainability, civic engagement, and robotics. The Banatao Institute is the UC-wide arm.

Key AI Areas:

  • AI for Health
  • AI for Sustainability and Climate
  • Robotics for Societal Applications
  • Data Analytics for Social Good
  • Policy and Ethics of AI/IT

Notable Faculty/Leaders: Costas Spanos (Director), Camille Crittenden (Executive Director). Many affiliated faculty leverage CITRIS resources.

Major Projects/Labs/Collaborations: Operates the CITRIS Invention Lab, CITRIS Foundry incubator, supports cross-disciplinary research projects, engages in policy initiatives (e.g., CITRIS Policy Lab).

Website: https://citris-uc.org/
